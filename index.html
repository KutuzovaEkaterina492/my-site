1 January the first month of the year in the Julian and Gregorian calendars, the eleventh month of the Old Roman year, which began before Caesar's reform in March. One of seven months with a length of 31 days. This is, on average, the coldest month of the year in most of the Northern Hemisphere of the Earth (where January is the second month of winter), and the warmest month of the year in most of the Southern Hemisphere
2 February the second month in the Julian and Gregorian calendars, the twelfth month of the Old Roman year, which began before Caesar's reform in March. This is the shortest month of the year, and the only one with fewer than 30 days: it has 28 days in a normal year and 29 days in leap years. In the Northern hemisphere of the Earth it is the third month of winter, in the Southern hemisphere it is the third month of summer.
3 March the third month of the year in the Julian and Gregorian calendars, the first month of the Old Roman year, which began before Caesar's reform in March. One of seven months with a length of 31 days. In the Northern Hemisphere of the Earth, March is the first month of spring, in the Southern hemisphere - the first month of autumn.
4 April the fourth month of the year in the Julian and Gregorian calendars, the second month of the Old Roman year, which began before Caesar's reform in March. One of four months with a length of 30 days.
